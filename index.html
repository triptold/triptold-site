Why Triptold·Not a database. Not an agency.

Not a database. Not an agency. Something built differently.

Most platforms optimise for scale. We optimise for trust. Here is what that means in practice.

01
Editorial matching, not search filters
Every creator recommendation includes written reasoning explaining why they fit, what audience trust they hold, and what story they are uniquely positioned to tell. A shortlist of 5 to 8 people, not a database of thousands.
02
Emotional territory, not follower count
A creator with 12,000 deeply engaged followers in the right territory outperforms a 200,000 generalist audience. We find people whose audiences already trust the kind of experience you offer.
03
Agency-quality support at platform economics
Contracts, compliance, briefing, content guidance, reporting. The work of a good creative agency, structured as a platform. You pay less. You get more. The administrative burden disappears entirely.
04
Bookings you can actually measure
For the first time, you can connect a creator's story directly to real bookings. Not impressions. Not reach. Actual stays. We measure what matters, over a timeframe that reflects how people actually make travel decisions.
05
Compliance handled properly, not left to chance
Paid partnerships must be disclosed. Contracts must be signed. Most brands and creators handle this inconsistently or not at all. On Triptold, disclosure, contracts and payment are handled properly as standard. You focus on the work. We handle the paperwork.
06
Intelligence that compounds over time
Every brief, every match, every booking outcome is logged. This builds proprietary insight about which creator and brand combinations actually drive travel decisions. The longer the network runs, the sharper it becomes.
"We are inspired by destinations. We make our decisions because of people."
Why we built this

Travel has become strangely empty.

Thousands of beautiful videos, perfect drone shots and hidden gems. Yet people still arrive disappointed because the story did not match the reality. Travel decisions carry enormous emotional weight. People are investing time, money, hope and memory into experiences they cannot get back.

A bad jumper is annoying. A bad holiday can become a lost anniversary, wasted savings, stress with your children, or precious time you cannot get back. People are not buying travel logically. They are buying anticipation, identity, connection, rest and escape. The stories shaping those decisions matter enormously.

We also think people are starting to feel the disconnect. Much of modern travel content is driven by incentives that have little to do with genuine experience. Follower counts can be inflated, audiences can be passive, and popularity is not the same thing as trust.

"The problem is not lack of content. It is lack of meaning. Triptold exists because the right storyteller can make somebody feel a place before they get there. We want to put trust back into the marketing mix."

That is what we are building. Deliberately, carefully, and with the right people from the start.
